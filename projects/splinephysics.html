Physics Based Train Movement Along a Spline for TrainTD

Project Overview

This was one of my first YouTube videos, and didn't know exactly where I wanted to go with it, but it turned into a timelapse/tutorial video that explained how I built the physics spline train mechanic. For the TrainTD game, I wanted to make a train that you could control and make it feel similar to how real train physics would act. I initially considered modelling a real train simulation using tracks with colliders, and wheels on the train would react with them, however I quickly realized this would bring the project way out of scope.

Instead, I dove into working with splines. The problem with this was that pretty much all resources I could find used t-based animation to move objects along the spline, so having objects move in response to physical forces didn't really work. To solve this problem, I had to come up with my own solution to make objects moving along the spline have their own velocity that is constantly changing its direction based on the current spline orientation, but keeping its magnitude the same based on external forces. I go over how I worked through these challenges in this video.
