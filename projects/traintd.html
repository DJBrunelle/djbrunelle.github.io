Train Tower Defense

Project Overview

TrainTD is similar to a Tower Defense game like BloonsTD, but instead of placing towers on a map around a road, you are in control of a train that has the towers as train carts moving along a train track. Your job is to build up the train and fight off enemies trying to attack the train and steal the money you are collecting. The progression of the game works similarly to Age of War, where you start in the "sticks and stones" era, and slowly work your way up through medieval era, modern era, futuristic, etc.

Development Notes

This has been one of the larger projects I have worked on, and it's been very satisfying putting together elaborate systems that are well designed and work together seamlessly.

Building the train mechanics was a highlight of this project, and I covered this in depth in one of my YouTube videos.

A large part of this project was developing all of the most important systems and how they would communicate with eachother. I spent several days planning this out, and caught a planning session in a timelapse.

My Reflections

  • As one of my larger projects, learning how to manage code and assets as they grow was a lesson I learned along the way. As we added new assets almost daily, it started getting hard to remember where everything was. This resulted in changing the project hierarchy a couple time as we progressed through the project. Even with more thoughtful organization, a large project will be inherently difficult to navigate, and trade offs will always be needed.
  • Managing a group of people requires very clear communication, and agreed upon processes. This was this groups first time working on a longer term project, and there were constant improvements we needed to make in order for things to work well. Git best practices had to be agreed upon, everyone had to be kept up to date on the vision of the game, tasks and dependencies needed to be managed, and much more. This was a difficult but very helpful learning experience
  • Having a more hasty prototyping phase could be helpful in the future. We spent several months on this prototype before we came to the conclusion that it would be difficult to turn what we're making into a full game that was fun to play and had clear objectives. Although I am very happy with the experience I gained from this project, it would be much more efficient going forward if we didn't put so much work into polishing a game before we know for sure that it will be a game we want to complete.
