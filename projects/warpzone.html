WarpZone

Project Overview

Warpzone happened as part of the Scorespace game jam where the theme was "Protect", and all games needed to have a score tracker and leaderboard. WarpZone became a 2D space shooter with a station in the middle that you had to protect from aliens. You are controlling a ship that shoots lasers, and you need to attack incoming aliens going straight for the station. There are powerups and endless waves with increasing difficulty. There is a persistent leaderboard which uses an central server to keep track of everyones scores.

Development Notes

We developed this as a team of three: a 2D Artist, a Sound Designer, and me as the developer.

As my first game jam, I took a lot away from this project. I spent a lot of time designing the ship system and trying to make the code reusable. Of course, the time crunch always catches up in game jams, and it got a bit messy. But overall, this was a success and still is one of my favourite games I've made in terms of design.

My Reflections

  • The controls are pretty unintuitive in hindsight. Having all the keys (WASD) change relative to where the ship is to the mouse doesn't feel completely right. As the developer, I got used to this really quickly and didn't notice how unintuitive it was until we started getting feedback from the players. This quickly highlighted the importance of playtesting for me!
