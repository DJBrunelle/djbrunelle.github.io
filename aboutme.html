Daniel Brunelle

Programmer passionate about making games

Introduction:

Hello! I'm Daniel, a passionate and driven game developer on a quest to create immersive and enjoyable gaming experiences. My journey into the world of game development began at a young age when I marveled at the vast and captivating landscapes of games like RuneScape. I couldn't help but wonder how these intricate worlds were brought to life within a web browser.

Educational Background:

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science with a minor in Business, providing me with a strong foundation in both the technical and entrepreneurial aspects of the industry. I incorporated Paridot Games in October of 2022, and have since been awarded $15,000 in funding, have made several YouTube videos on behalf of the company, and participated in several projects.

Technical Proficiencies:

My skill set includes a deep understanding of programming languages such as C++ and C#, along with hands-on experience with popular game development tools like Unity. I'm well-versed in version control using Git and proficient in project management tools that ensure smooth development processes. I've also implemented key design principles, including the observer pattern, command pattern, and singletons, to create efficient and engaging game systems. Moreover, I have a firm grasp of game loops, update methods, and event queues.

Exprience and Leadership:

As a game developer, I have taken on leadership roles in numerous game jams, guiding teams to success and ensuring the completion of games with complete and compelling gameplay loops. I've also created informative YouTube tutorials on niche programming topics, sharing my knowledge with others in the game development community.

Passion for Building and Simulation Games:

My favorite games, including Satisfactory, RuneScape, Kerbal Space Program, and Minecraft, reflect my passion for simulation and building experiences. I consistently strive to incorporate crafting and building mechanics into my projects, although I acknowledge the challenges of managing scope in such ambitious endeavors.

Community Involvement:

I'm an active member of the game development community, with a special dedication to the non-profit organization GameCamp Edmonton. I've volunteered at events, including representing the organization at out-of-town conventions like Gamecon Canada. Regularly attending keynotes and networking events keeps me informed about industry trends and fosters valuable connections.

Commitment to Learning:

I'm constantly evolving my skill set and staying up-to-date with the latest industry trends and technologies. When faced with new challenges or opportunities, I make it a point to dedicate time to learning and often share my newfound knowledge through informative YouTube videos.

Mission and Vision:

Ultimately, my mission as a game developer is to bring joy to people through my creations, much like the joy that games have brought to me over the years. I'm excited to continue my journey, develop captivating games, and share them with players around the world. Thank you for taking the time to explore my portfolio, and I look forward to the exciting adventures that lie ahead in the world of game development.

Some of my Favourite Games

Satisfactory

As the first factory game I've played, Satisfactory immediately had me in its grasp with beautiful landscapes and incredibly satisfying logistics problems.

RuneScape

RuneScape was my first MMORPG, and one of my first games in general. I played it for years starting back in 2005, and it has shaped the way I experience and evaluate games

Rust

Rust was both an amazing gameplay and bonding experience. Getting dropped in a huge map with nothing but a rock and friends to survive among 100 other people was an unforgettable experience.

Kerbal Space Program

This game taught me almost everything I know about rockets and space flight. I can watch real rocket launch livestreams and understand most of what they are talking about because of KSP.

Roller Coaster Tycoon

I received this in a cereal box in the early 2000's, which proceeded to provide me with 100's of hours of coercing guests onto dangerous rollercoasters and other regular amusement park management activities.

Minecraft

Although I didn't play Minecraft until the late 2010's, I spent countlesss hours making redstone circuits and building skytrains to my friends bases for thoroughly sufficient transportation among the server.
